The value that is the most important to me is Confidence. I have always believed that life is 30% of what you do and 70% on how you react to it and having confidence will not only be useful for that first 30%, but prepares you for that other 70%. I believe that everything in life starts with confidence and if you don’t have confidence in yourself, you will not enjoy or succeed at anything in life. Confidence is not a trait, it is a mindset. I know it is rash, but if you have self-confidence, you have the ability to accomplish anything you set your mind to.

What was the last topic that someone asked for your advice on?

The last topic someone ask my advice on is how I have the ability to stay cool and collected under pressure. I have always been good at keeping calm even in the most stressful times; maybe it is because I have pitched in front of thousands of yelling fans all throughout my college baseball career and have never let anyone get under my skin. I have always had that sort of calmness about me. My solution to someone asking me how I do this, is ask WHY? What’s the point of getting all worked up about something that you cannot change and is out of your control. It is a personal feeling and you control your feelings, so why worry about what you can’t control and never let things beyond your control influence your life.

How do you feel when you think about your values? Do you think it can help you mediate stereotype threat if you recognize you feel it?

When I think about my values, I feel proud. I am proud of who I have become and how I feel about myself. Obviously I have my doubts and have made my mistakes, but I know that nothing can stop me in becoming the person I want to become. In regards to stereotype threat, I can’t and won’t let anyone else tell me how I should live my life. I am ultimately in control of my own future and the only person who can influence me is myself. When I am feeling down, I just tell myself to think back and remember everything I have accomplished up to this point and in every one of those situations the real reason I had truly felt satisfied is because I had to fight for what I wanted.
