Pair Programming

By: Matt Evanoff and Elliott Young

What is Pair Programming?

Pair Programming is two people working in tandem on the same problem to double their efficiency and brainpower.

Why is Pair Programming important?

Pair Programming is important because it allows each teammate helps the other out, which is especially useful for novice programmers who are still learning the ropes of coding.

Compare the Driver and the Navigator

The primary difference between the driver and the navigator is that one, the navigator, will direct and dictate what code should be written to the driver who will type it. Even in cases where the driver isn't exactly correct, the driver will type what they say, allowing them to learn from the mistakes.
